FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Not sure about something? Need to find out more? Other than the tutorial page, here is your best bet. If you're still not sure, feel free to contact us.

Common Questions

Life Calendar is helpful by providing a minimal, yet aesthetic and clear place to see your life's direction. By tracking what you've done over periods of your life, I firmly believe that it is beneficial by encouraging you to make use of the time you have. Likewise, you can see how you've progressed in hobbies or aspects of your life over time, stay accountable by creating goals for yourself, and use it as your personal diary.
No, you do not need an account to be able to use Life Calendar. You can still access your life calendar and statistics page. However, all of your information will disappear once your computer restarts or you close the browser!

While it may good to use an anonymous account for a couple minutes to see how it looks, we highly recommend you register. This will allow you to have your data securely stored with us and you can login everywhere.
The ability to color-code buttons is one of the most critical features of Life Calendar. You can select a button number on the home page, and then the color to shade/unshade it with, and the button will magically get a stripe (or have it removed) from that color!

The purpose of this is for you to color various boxes based on key, repeated events that happened throughout your life or things you've done. For example, you could color the life box for that day with a light purple bar if you went out with friends, or dark brown if you were sick.

I recommend sticking to about a maximum of nine colors per box, because beyond that you won't be able to see the stripes (even though it will be saved to the legend).
The statistics page is a fun little web page that shows you some details about your life and usage of Life Calendar. You can skip over this entirely or check it every once in a while to see how you've been doing. It includes statistics such as when you registered, what percent of the way you are through your life, how many words you've inputted to boxes, what colors you use most frequently, and a lot more.
Because of the intensely personal nature of Life Calendar - this is meant to be more of a personal diary than social media site - there is no dedicated social media integration. However, you can download or print your personalized life calendar at the download page or take screenshots of your statistics to compare with others.
Despite costs associated with the domain name, web hosting, and database storage, at the moment Life Calendar is 100% free. We may introduce premium plans in the future to help cover the costs associated with app maintenance, but if that ever does happen you will still have access to all of your information.
Financial support is more than welcome to help keep the project going, supporting its users and opening their eyes to their lives. You can donate on GitHub.

Donations through PayPal are also supported and welcome.
If you're still not sure how things work, you can take a look at the text tutorial or hop onto YouTube to see the video demo.

Advanced Questions


On the goals page, you can set goals for yourself and hold yourself accountable to them. You can give the goals title, subtitles, and some text, and then a due date and due time. Although you do not receive an email when the due date expires, you can still shade the goals (in progress, cancelled, etc.) and visually overview all the major goals you've gone through in your life.

You can read a bit more in the tutorial here.
The "legend" is the area above the home page where you can view all of the colors that you use to color-code your buttons. The primary benefit of the legend is that you can modify it to save the meanings of each of the colors that you use to modify your life calendar boxes with.

You can also use the legend to find colors and meaning that you have used before, with their color codes - if you click on the color as well, it will copy to the color picker.

You can read a bit more in the tutorial here.
While most may use Fancy Mode for creating life calendars, there is an option to use Markdown. Markdown is one of the simplest formatting languages out there yet likewise one of the most popular, without any of the extra fluff. You can create tables, italiczed text, and more simply from monospaced font.

Examples:
  • *italicize*
  • **bold**
  • :heart:
  • [google link](https://www.google.com)


It's completely fine do go on using the Fancy Mode, but if you're a programmer or want to be even more minimalist you can use the Markdown Mode. A great guide can be accessed here.
Because of the way that Markdown is built, you might have some trouble moving between it and Fancy Mode - I recommend using only one or the other for each box. Fancy Mode uses dynamically generated HTML and images, which often display as raw when converted to HTML. However, it is safe to move from Markdown to Fancy Mode.
We know that you trust us to store what's most important to you - your memories. At Life Calendar, we take that trust very seriously. Not even we can see your data, and your password is securely encrypted and stored in our off-site database.

Tech Questions

Yes! Life Calendar is open source. It was written predominantly in Python and JavaScript, with the Flask library and Jinja templating. The GitHub repository is over here.
Currently, Life Calendar has no plans of coming to mobile - a mobile app is a whole new ball game. However, the website is mobile optimized and you can visit our site just as easily on a mobile web browser.
The site does use an application programming interface (API) to handle user box requests. However, it's pretty bare-bones at the moment and most of the routes are protected and require a dedicated login.

If there is interest in the future, however, we will strongly consider implementing an API to interact with the database. This would bring benefits for tech-savvy users, by allowing them to program integration with Google Calendar, emails, etc.
No one likes bugs! If you found a bug, please make sure to let us know! You can open an issue on Github or shoot us an email.

If you think you can solve the issue, even better! You can open a pull request at the repo.
